This repo was created as a workspace for the **Fiduciary Agent Stack Task Force** of the Trust Over IP AIM Working Group. The purpose of this Task Force is to create an Agent-to Agent Stack particularly for long term or consequential relationships bound by mutual duties both specific and general. This Task Force is the result of Mr. Vitka receiving a warm reception to his proposal, which is discussed here: https://github.com/trustoverip/dtgwg-general/discussions/21

Below are some links to **Stephen Vitka's Fiduciary Agent Series**, that forms the starting documentation of this repo. In it he describes the evolution of his thinking about fiduciary agents: 

The basic idea: AI agents should not merely be helpful, persuasive, autonomous, or aligned in the abstract. The agents that mediate human life should owe duties. They should know whom they serve (their beneficiaries), what they are allowed to do, what they are forbidden to do, when they must ask permission, what records they must keep, and how their mistakes can be corrected.

These are coming to be called  fiduciary agents, but I prefer, simply, fid-agents. A fid-agent is not just a chatbot with better manners. It is not merely a model trained on a constitution, or prompted with a persona file.  A real fid-agent is a relationship-level system: duty-bound, capability-bounded, workflow-checked, reviewable, and accountable to a beneficiary and other principals. 

My eight-paper series (so far!) is inspired by members of the GliaNet Alliance, Kwaai, ToIP, and others, develops the idea from moral intuition to architecture, law, strategy, and implementation. 

The paper series and how my thinking has evolved: ADD LINKS
1. Personally-Sovereign Digital Twins:
The foundational manifesto. Externally-facing personally-sovereign AI should represent a person’s aspirational self, not their worst impulses or hidden cravings. The earliest form of duty appears as “Be Excellent To”: a universal behavioral constraint that later becomes the formal Duty of Care.
2. Taxonomy for Agent Systems:
An attempt to define ambiguous industry terms so that unambiguous entities can act on each other through gated boundaries.  It separates Model, Workspace, Workflow, Agent(Live), Agent Role, and Bot/Robot, so that generation, interpretation, and execution can be separated, audited, and constrained such that implementing duties reliably is even possible. 
3. Kwaai’s Personal Agency / VASTI (Virtual Assistant and Sliding Tabs Interface):
The user-sovereign interface through which the user can confidently engage with fid-agents. Instead of letting corporate platforms own our data, context, attention, and digital agency, this paper describes a personal AI stack where the user regains control of their digital environment. 
4. Taxonomy of Duties for Net Fiduciaries:
The legal and institutional grammar. “Be excellent To” becomes an exhaustive hierarchical agent duty stack: care, loyalty, privacy, transparency, consent, confidentiality, corrigibility, recourse, standardization, contextual interpretation and so much more
5. AI Will Not Replace Us:
Personally owned digital twins and working with their owners and employer fid-agents can make humans more capable, trusted, creative, and irreplaceable, which is better than replacing them with brittle full automation.
6. Fiduciary Preferences:
The bridge to implementation. Agents should not act from the beneficiary’s raw desire, inferred behavior, platform incentives, or arbitrary generated preferences. They should act from their beneficiary’s professed preferences transformed through duties into fiduciary preferences. Includes ceremonies of cooperation and description of mutual Verifiable Relationship Records  to record interactions.
7. Verified Fiduciary Reciprocity:
The attractor of global alignment. A society of trustworthy agents cannot be based on naive niceness. It must cooperate preferentially with agents that can prove fiduciary control, provenance, bounded capabilities, auditability, recourse, and reputational exposure.
8. Fast Tracking Fid-Agents:
The  pre-implementation paper. We cannot wait for perfect legal, cryptographic, and standards infrastructure. We must build provisional fid-agents now: narrow, useful, constrained, human-reviewed, and precedent-producing.
